# Advisors & Research Toolkit

Who to call, when, and how to be a good client — plus the general research
sources that serve every domain.

## The advisor map

| Advisor type | What they're for | Call them when |
|---|---|---|
| **Crypto-specialist regulatory counsel** | Perimeter, licensing strategy, decentralization analysis, regulator contact | Before launch in any market; immediately on any Red rating in domains 1, 7, 10 |
| **Securities / token counsel** | Token classification, fundraising instruments, public-sale exposure | Before issuing or promising any token; before any raise using token-linked instruments |
| **Corporate / startup counsel** | Entity structure, cap table, contracts, data-room discipline | At formation; before any restructuring; before diligence |
| **AML / financial-crime consultant** | AML program design, KYC/monitoring/sanctions setup, remediation, MLRO support | Before onboarding the first user; this week, if users are live without a program |
| **Tax advisor (crypto-experienced)** | Structure tax, token treasury, grants, user reporting regimes | Before token issuance; before an exit process; when entities span borders |
| **Privacy / data-protection counsel or DPO service** | GDPR-grade review, privacy policy vs. reality, vendor agreements | When EU/UK users exist; before diligence |
| **Smart-contract security firm** | Audits, admin-key/access-control review (the on-chain half of domain 7) | Before mainnet; after any privileged-function change |
| **Custody / wallet-infrastructure specialist** | Key-management architecture, custodian selection | When the product first touches user funds or keys |
| **Fractional compliance officer** | Owning the license map, product-governance gate, policies that match reality | From first live users until a full-time hire makes sense |
| **Audit / assurance firm** | Financial audit, proof-of-reserves-style reconciliation assurance | When client-asset balances become material; pre-exit |
| **Licensing consultant / local licensed counsel** | Drafting and shepherding license applications | When the licensing strategy picks a jurisdiction |
| **Foundation / DAO structuring specialist** | Ring-fencing structures for decentralized projects | Only after regulatory counsel says a structure is the right tool |

## Sequencing when everything is urgent

A founder can't hire twelve advisors. The dependency order that usually
holds:

1. **Perimeter counsel first** — until you know what you are and where,
   every other engagement is guessing at scope.
2. **AML consultant immediately if users are live** — the only domain where
   time-with-users directly accumulates exposure.
3. **Token/securities counsel before anything token irreversible** —
   issuance, promises, or public sale.
4. Everything else sequences off the triage report's Red/Amber ratings.

## Being a good client (making advice 10× cheaper)

- **Bring facts, not vibes.** The research paths in the domain files exist
  so the founder arrives with the entity diagram, user-country data, wallet
  map, and instrument register. Advisors billing hourly to assemble facts
  the startup already had is the most common waste of legal spend.
- **Ask for the decision, not the memo.** "Given these facts, which of
  these three paths, and what would change your answer?" beats "please
  analyze our regulatory position" — in cost and usefulness.
- **Sequence for privilege.** If something looks genuinely bad (past public
  sale, sanctioned user), talk to counsel *before* writing internal analyses
  or postmortems.
- **Specialist means specialist.** Ask any candidate advisor for their last
  three crypto engagements. Generalist counsel confidently wrong about
  crypto perimeter questions is a recurring cause of Red ratings.

## General research toolkit

Per-domain research paths live in the domain files. Sources that serve
every domain:

- **Regulator primary sources** — perimeter guidance, licensing pages, and
  warning lists from the FCA (UK), ESMA/national authorities under MiCA
  (EU), SEC/CFTC/FinCEN and state regulators (US), MAS (Singapore), VARA
  (Dubai), the Israel Securities Authority and Capital Market Authority
  (Israel), among others. Always prefer the regulator's own page over
  commentary.
- **FATF** — the Recommendations and Virtual Asset guidance are the global
  AML baseline; national regimes rhyme with them.
- **Block explorers + audit reports** — for verifying on-chain claims
  (admin keys, treasury flows) rather than trusting documentation.
- **The Wayback Machine and platform archives** — for the marketing-history
  self-audit; diligence teams use them, so use them first.
- **Regulator enforcement actions** — reading a few actions against
  similar business models is the fastest education in what a regulator
  actually cares about.

Treat law-firm client alerts and industry-association explainers as maps,
not territory: good for orientation, never a substitute for the primary
source or for counsel.
